Cybersecurity Breaches and Phishing Email Identification
Practical Report
1. Introduction
Cybersecurity breaches occur when attackers gain unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or data. These breaches often result in the theft of sensitive information, financial loss, and damage to an organization’s reputation. In recent years, cyberattacks have increased significantly due to vulnerabilities in software, human errors, and sophisticated hacking techniques.

This report examines recent cybersecurity breaches, their causes, and impacts, and demonstrates how phishing emails can be identified using free online tools.

2. Recent Cybersecurity Breaches
2.1 Snowflake Data Breach (2024)
Overview:
The Snowflake data breach involved unauthorized access to cloud environments used by many companies worldwide.

Cause:

Weak authentication practices

Compromised login credentials

Lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA)

Attackers used stolen credentials to access company databases hosted on the cloud. 

Impact:

Data from over 100 organizations was compromised

Exposure of customer data from major companies

Loss of trust in cloud security systems

Financial and legal consequences for affected companies

2.2 Qantas Airline Data Breach (2025)
Overview:
A cyberattack on Qantas exposed personal information of up to 6 million customers.

Cause:

Compromise of a third-party platform used by the airline’s contact center

Possible social engineering techniques used by attackers. 

Impact:

Exposure of customer names, emails, phone numbers, and birth dates

Risk of identity theft and phishing scams

Investigation by cybersecurity authorities

2.3 ManageMyHealth Data Breach (2025)
Overview:
The ManageMyHealth online patient portal in New Zealand suffered unauthorized access that exposed sensitive health information.

Cause:

Unauthorized system access by attackers

Possible security vulnerabilities in the patient portal.

Impact:

More than 400,000 medical documents stolen

Over 120,000 patients affected

Government investigations and privacy concerns. 

2.4 Kido International Ransomware Attack (2025)
Overview:
A ransomware attack targeted Kido International, an early-education organization operating nurseries.

Cause:

Malware infection leading to ransomware deployment

Weak network security controls.

Impact:

Personal data of around 8,000 children and staff leaked

Sensitive data such as photos, addresses, and contact details exposed

Major concerns about child safety and privacy. 

3. Common Causes of Cybersecurity Breaches
From the analyzed incidents, the main causes include:

Weak Passwords or Credential Theft
Attackers exploit reused passwords or stolen login credentials.

Phishing and Social Engineering
Hackers trick users into revealing sensitive information.

Software Vulnerabilities
Unpatched software or zero-day vulnerabilities allow attackers to exploit systems.

Third-Party Security Risks
Many breaches occur through vendors or service providers connected to company systems.

Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Without additional verification, attackers can easily access accounts.

4. Impact of Cybersecurity Breaches
Cybersecurity breaches can cause serious consequences:

4.1 Financial Loss
Organizations may lose millions through fraud, ransom payments, and system recovery costs.

4.2 Data Theft
Sensitive data such as:

Personal information

Medical records

Financial details
may be stolen.

4.3 Reputation Damage
Customers may lose trust in companies after a data breach.

4.4 Legal Consequences
Organizations may face lawsuits, regulatory fines, and government investigations.

4.5 Operational Disruption
Some attacks shut down systems and services, affecting normal business operations.

5. Phishing Email Identification
Phishing emails are fraudulent messages designed to trick users into revealing passwords, financial information, or installing malware.

Common Signs of Phishing Emails
Suspicious sender email address

Urgent or threatening messages

Unexpected attachments or links

Requests for passwords or personal data

Poor grammar or unusual formatting

6. Simulation Using Free Online Tools
Several free tools can help identify phishing emails.

6.1 Google Safe Browsing
Website:
https://transparencyreport.google.com/safe-browsing/search

Use:
Check if a suspicious link in an email leads to a malicious website.

6.2 VirusTotal
Website:
https://www.virustotal.com

Use:

Scan email links or attachments

Detect malware or phishing URLs

6.3 PhishTank
Website:
https://www.phishtank.com

Use:

Verify whether a suspicious email link is a known phishing site.

Example Phishing Email Analysis
Email Message Example

Subject: Urgent! Your Bank Account Will Be Suspended

Message:
"Dear customer, your account has been temporarily blocked. Click the link below immediately to verify your account."

Analysis

Indicators of phishing:

Generic greeting (“Dear customer”)

Urgent message

Suspicious verification link

Request for sensitive information

Conclusion: Likely phishing email

7. Conclusion
Cybersecurity breaches continue to increase globally, affecting businesses, governments, and individuals. Most breaches occur due to weak authentication, phishing attacks, and software vulnerabilities. Organizations must adopt stronger cybersecurity practices such as multi-factor authentication, regular system updates, and employee awareness training.

Phishing detection tools such as VirusTotal, PhishTank, and Google Safe Browsing can help users identify malicious emails and prevent cyberattacks.
